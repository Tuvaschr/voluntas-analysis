Distribution of Income Source by Age Group

Grouped bar chart — counts per income source within each age group

Key observations

  • Employment is the most common income source across all age groups, ranging from 23.5% (25-34) to 31.9% (45-54) within each group.
  • "No income" is most prevalent among 35-44 year olds (13.2%) and lowest among 45-54 (4.3%) and 55+ (4.5%).
  • Self-employment peaks among 25-34 year olds (26.5%) and 55+ (27.3%), and is lowest in the 45-54 group (17.0%).
  • Family/remittances is highest among the youngest group (18-24, 23.1%) and declines with age to 15.9% among 55+.

Barrier Selection Rate by Gender

% of respondents within each gender selecting each barrier

Key observations

  • Long wait times is the most frequently reported barrier overall (41.0%, n=123) and shows the largest gender gap — females at 45.3% vs males at 36.0%, a difference of 9.3 percentage points.
  • Cost is the second most common barrier (39.0%, n=117), with females slightly more likely to report it (41.0%) than males (36.7%), a gap of 4.3 percentage points.
  • Distance and language are both reported more by males: distance at 31.7% vs 25.5% (gap: 6.2pp), language at 21.6% vs 17.4% (gap: 4.2pp).
  • Lack of awareness is the least commonly reported barrier for both genders (females 16.8%, males 20.1%), with males slightly more likely to select it (gap: 3.3pp).
  • Females report higher rates on 2 of 5 barriers (cost, long wait times); males report higher rates on 3 of 5 (distance, language, lack of awareness).
